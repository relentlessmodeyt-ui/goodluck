Conditions treated by our specialists

The cardiology team at Apex Hospital Malviya Nagar Jaipur treats the full range of heart and vascular conditions. We focus on acute presentations and long-term chronic disease management so patients receive seamless care in Jaipur and the surrounding region.

  • Coronary artery disease (CAD) and angina: caused by atherosclerosis (plaque buildup) in coronary arteries leading to chest pain, breathlessness and reduced exercise tolerance. Treatments include medical therapy, PCI/angioplasty, and CABG.
  • Acute myocardial infarction (heart attack): sudden blockage of coronary artery causing chest pain, sweating, nausea and shortness of breath. Immediate reperfusion (primary PCI) is the standard of care; our 24-hour heart hospital Malviya Nagar Jaipur is equipped for emergency PCI.
  • Heart failure: symptoms of fatigue, breathlessness, orthopnea and leg swelling due to impaired heart function. Management includes guideline-directed medical therapy, device therapy (CRT), and multidisciplinary cardiac rehabilitation.
  • Valvular heart disease: stenosis or regurgitation of mitral, aortic or other valves causing breathlessness, palpitations and reduced tolerance. Options include valve repair, replacement and transcatheter interventions where appropriate.
  • Arrhythmias: palpitations, syncope or irregular pulse due to conditions such as atrial fibrillation, SVT or ventricular arrhythmias. Treatments include medications, ablation procedures and implantable devices (pacemakers/ICD).
  • Congenital heart disease (pediatric and adult): structural heart defects present from birth. We manage and perform device closures or corrective surgeries for appropriate cases.
  • Cardiomyopathies and myocarditis: muscle diseases of the heart causing reduced pumping function and heart failure; treatments range from medications to advanced therapies.
  • Peripheral vascular disease and pulmonary hypertension: vascular conditions that affect circulation and pulmonary pressures and often require specialized multidisciplinary care.

For each condition we explain causes, symptoms and evidence-based options. For example, coronary artery disease is primarily caused by modifiable risk factors—high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, smoking, sedentary lifestyle and obesity—so treatment often combines lifestyle interventions, medications and revascularization when needed.

Treatment process at our hospital

At Apex Hospital cardiology Jaipur the patient journey is designed to be transparent, efficient and patient-centred. Whether you come for a routine heart check, an elective procedure or emergency heart attack treatment in Jaipur, the process follows clear steps to ensure safety and quality.

1. Initial assessment and diagnostics

Patients are evaluated in outpatient clinics or emergency triage. Non-invasive tests available on-site include ECG, 2D echocardiography, treadmill test (TMT), Holter monitoring, and advanced cardiac imaging when required. Our diagnostic teams provide rapid reporting to accelerate decision-making.

2. Multidisciplinary evaluation and shared decision-making

Complex cases are discussed in the integrated heart team, which includes interventional cardiologists, cardiac surgeons and cardiac intensivists. We explain treatment alternatives, risks and expected recovery to help patients make informed choices.

3. Pre-procedure optimization and consent

Prior to procedures such as angioplasty or CABG we optimize comorbid conditions (diabetes, hypertension) and perform necessary pre-op investigations. Informed consent includes details about the procedure, potential complications and postoperative care expectations.

4. Procedure and immediate recovery

Our advanced hybrid Cath Lab and operating rooms enable safe execution of interventions. Post-procedure, patients are monitored in the cardiac ICU which provides continuous hemodynamic monitoring, ventilatory support if needed and expert critical care.

5. Rehabilitation and follow-up

Cardiac rehabilitation programs—exercise training, medication titration, dietary counselling, and psychosocial support—are integral for long-term success. We schedule structured follow-up visits and provide telephone/WhatsApp support at 9829030011 to address queries or medication issues.

When to consult a specialist?

Knowing when to consult a cardiologist is crucial. Some symptoms require immediate emergency attention while others warrant prompt outpatient evaluation. If you are in Malviya Nagar Jaipur or nearby and experience any of the following, contact our emergency cardiology services immediately at 9829030011 or visit the hospital:

  • New or severe chest pain or pressure, especially radiating to jaw, arm or back
  • Sudden breathlessness, difficulty breathing or shortness of breath at rest
  • Syncope (fainting) or near-fainting episodes
  • Sudden onset of palpitations with dizziness or chest discomfort
  • Signs of heart failure—rapid weight gain, increasing leg swelling, or difficulty lying flat
  • Uncontrolled or severely high blood pressure, especially with headache or visual changes
  • Concerning screening results—high cholesterol, abnormal ECG or abnormal echocardiogram findings

For non-urgent concerns such as routine follow-up, risk factor management (hypertension, diabetes, cholesterol) or preventive heart check-ups, schedule an outpatient appointment. Apex Hospital offers preventive heart check-up packages and periodic diagnostic discounts for eligible patients. Early consultation helps reduce the risk of advanced disease and allows discussion of lifestyle modification, medical therapy and, when indicated, timely interventions.

2. How long is the hospital stay after angioplasty?

Most uncomplicated angioplasty patients stay 24–72 hours for observation and discharge instructions. Individual factors—vascular access site, contrast response and comorbidities—may require longer stays.

3. Can I use insurance to pay for surgery?

Yes. Apex Hospital works with major insurance providers, TPAs and government schemes. We provide documentation and help with pre-authorization and claims. Contact our billing team for details and package options.

4. What lifestyle changes are recommended after stent placement or bypass surgery?

Key recommendations include smoking cessation, heart-healthy diet, regular exercise as advised by cardiac rehab, strict control of diabetes and hypertension and adherence to prescribed medications (antiplatelets, statins, beta blockers etc.). 5. Do you perform pediatric cardiology and congenital heart procedures?

6. What are the risks of bypass surgery?

CABG is a major surgery; potential risks include bleeding, infection, stroke, heart rhythm disturbances and complications related to comorbidities. Our preoperative assessment aims to minimize these risks and the heart team discusses individualized risk-benefit profiles.
